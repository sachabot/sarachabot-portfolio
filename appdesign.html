Graditap - App Design

Overview

In my SMAD 317 User Interaction Design course, I worked on a semester long group project with five other UX/UI Designers to create a mockup app called "Graditap." Since this course was offered during the midst of the pandemic, we decided to create a communication app which allowed people to use different modes of senses such as touch, sight, and hearing. We thought this app would help those who were impacted by Covid-19 to better connect and share meaningful messages with their loved ones from even from a distance.


For our approach of designing this app, we conducted UX research that included, comparative analysis, interviews, data analysis, storyboards, user journey maps, personas, information architecture, wireframes, low and high-fidelity prototypes. We also used the iterative process to examine the effectiveness of our design by conducting usablity testing. These approach allowed us to create a mockup app that was thoroughly researched and designed based on the user's feedback.

Design Question

"How might we improve meaningful communication for people who want to quickly check in with distant loved ones?

User Research

01 Define Target Audience

The users that we recruited for the interview were mostly in their early twenties and the ages ranged from 19-46. However, the intended users for this app includes users who digitally communicate with their loved ones.

02 Interview Process

For the interview process, users were asked to complete a 45-60 minute interview regarding digital communication either in-person or a through a video call. Researchers used otter.ai or voice memo to take notes.

03 Data Analysis/Summary

In sum, most users communicated with their loved ones such as close friends/family on a daily or semi-weekly basis. Some reported that they communicated with large numbers of people at work, but had low amount of communication in their personal lives.
