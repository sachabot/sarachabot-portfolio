Cultural Campaign Proposal

Project Overview

The purpose of this campaign was to develop a strategic cross-cultural campaign proposal by implementing cultural theories and dimensions. Our group chose "Foraco International SA", a French water drilling service to be marketed in Nigeria to combat the water scarcity issue and the lack of government assistance in the country.

View the full report here

Introduction

For our cultural campaign proposal, we chose Foraco International S.A. to be marketed in Nigeria. The goal of this advertising campaign was to garner the support and awareness of the water scarcity issue that has been detrimental to Nigeria's population. Around 60 million Nigerians do not have access to water source and with the advent of global warming, Nigeria will continue to be negatively impacted by it without a proper solution. Thus, we chose Foraco, a French water drilling company to aid Nigeria with increased water supply.

SWOT Analysis

The SWOT analysis displays the strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats to examine our approach and strategies of the campaign.

Strengths

  • Third largest mineral drillist in the world
  • Created national water schemes in Chad, Mali, and Libya
  • Best in-class safety standards with an international workforce trained for complex operations

    • Weaknesses

  • Negative impact on the quality of water due to mining
  • Small revenue for water drilling compared to standard mining
  • The small increase in revenue - indicating a small budget

    • Opportunities

  • Providing water drilling service to alleviate the population's concern for the lack of clean water
  • High media usage in the country = Cheap cost for social media advertising
  • Best in-class safety standards with an international workforce trained for complex operations

    • Threats

  • The potential threat of Boko Haram
  • The low usage of print media
  • A push back of the campaign due to a recent flood

    • Communication Objectives

    01 Objective (Educational)
    - To educate at least 50% of the target audience about the outcome of increased water access in the community from January 12th, 2023 to December 12th, 2024.

    02 Objective (Awareness)
    - To increase awareness of the brand and Foraco’s installation services towards 60% of the target audience starting on January 12th, 2023 to December 12th, 2024.

    03 Objective (Behavioral)
    - To increase the launch of water drilling programs among the target audiences through media advertising in Nigeria by 45%, starting on April 12th, 2023, to December 12th, 2024.

    Target Audience

    The target audience for this campaign will consist of Nigerian citizens as well as other West African countries surrounding Nigeria who are suffering from the drought. Nigeria in particular is targeted due to it being the most populated country in Africa, making it a cultural hub throughout the rest of the continent. Other surrounding nations in West Africa are likely to follow suit and also seek aid from Foraco to help with the water crisis.

    • Cultural Dimensions

    A situation analysis and cultural onion was created after researching the target audience's culture and applying Hofstede's cultural dimensions theory. Both of these graphs were essential to create a campaign that effectively applied the product to the other culture.

    Media Strategy

    Five media strategies selected for Foraco International S.A.’s campaign were (1) newspaper article, (2) magazine print advertisement, (3) Instagram advertisement, (4) Billboard, and (5) television advertisement. Based on the advertising spending in Nigeria, these forms of media were chosen to deliver the message that Foraco International S.A. can provide the means to increase the water supply and combat the water scarcity issue.

    Conclusion

    After researching the cultures and ideals of Nigeria, this campaign focuses on the long term benefits that Nigeria could reap through the increased water supply with the aid of Foraco International S.A. Several mediums will be used to promote the campaign, which was heavily researched to be accepted by the Nigerian culture. The slogan that we have chosen for this campaign was "Integrity. Innovation. Involvement." As Nigeria scores high on collectivism with a strong emphasis on taking responsibility for group members, the slogan holds true to the terms of Integrity and Involvement. The wells that will be placed in the community by Foraco will consider the collectivistic culture, as well as the long term benefits of health, indulgence, and longevity that the increased water supply could provide for Nigeria. The campaign efforts in Nigeria could spark attention in other neighboring countries like Chad where Foraco could provide solutions for the drought issues.
